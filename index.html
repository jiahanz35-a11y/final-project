Sun Life — Lumi (Design Jam)
Design Jam • Service Design

Lumi — A Virtual Caregiver for Chronic Patients

Problem: Fragmented chronic care, older patients overwhelmed with medication and appointments.

My role: UX research, service blueprint, onboarding flow, hi-fi screens.

Outcome: Prototype for hospital onboarding with Sun Life integration and trusted-contact system.

Problem space & Context

Ontario’s aging population and rising chronic illness rates place significant strain on hospitals. Patients like Ruth (67, diabetes + hypertension) often lack a coordinated care plan, leading to missed medication, repeated ER visits, and anxiety. Sun Life and Waterloo Hospital sought solutions to improve continuity, benefits understanding, and daily management.

Research

Methods: rapid interviews with stakeholders, service audit, claims data review (Sun Life reports), and competitor scan for digital chronic-care tools.

  • Interviews with seniors highlighted confusion around refills and transportation.
  • Staff interviews emphasized discharge friction and paperwork overload.

Key Insights

  • Human onboarding reduces tech anxiety.
  • Trusted contact increases adherence and reduces loneliness.
  • Syncing with benefits (Sun Life) and pharmacies reduces refill errors.

Persona

Ruth — Isolated Senior (67)

Lives alone, newly diagnosed with diabetes and hypertension. Overwhelmed by appointments, medication schedules, and hospital paperwork. Wants clarity and a trusted helper.

User flow

Hospital referral → Onboarding specialist → QR code link → App setup → Medication sync → App reminders & trusted contact updates.

Core Features

  • Human onboarding specialist (in-person first consult)
  • QR-based secure profile linking to hospital records
  • Medication schedule with Sun Life prescription sync
  • Appointment booking + transport coordination
  • Trusted contact updates and voice assistant

Service Blueprint (summary)

Hospital staff initiate referral → Sun Life onboarding team connects → Patient setup with QR → Ongoing digital reminders + human check-ins for 30 days → Escalation to care coordinator if missed medication/appointments.

Wireframes

Final UI / Prototype

Reflection

This rapid design jam reinforced the importance of starting with human trust — a design that introduces a human first dramatically increases adoption among older users. Future work: pilot at Waterloo Hospital and connect to live pharmacy refill APIs.

UW Stratford — Student Housing App
Project Sprint • UX

UW Haven — Rental Assistance for Students

Problem: Students struggle to find affordable, reliable housing quickly and manage leases and roommate communication.

My role: UX design, user research, prototyping.

Outcome: Prototype app for simplified search, verified listings, and roommate-matching tools.

Problem space

University students face housing shortages, scams, transport barriers, and difficulty understanding lease terms. Students off-campus (esp. international) are especially vulnerable.

Research & Methods

  • Surveys with 40 students on pain points
  • Competitive analysis of rental apps (Kijiji, Facebook Marketplace)
  • Expert interview with campus housing office

Key Findings

  • Trust & verification are top priorities
  • Students need flexible lease filters and transport-aware searches
  • Simple roommate matching reduced friction

Persona

Sam — International Student (21)

New to Canada, limited local network, seeks verified listings and clear lease guidance.

User Journey

Search → Verification & chat → Lease templates → Move-in checklist → Support resources

Key Features

  • Verified landlord badges
  • Map + transit-aware search
  • Lease guidance & templated messages
  • Roommate matching and split-bills helper

Impact Metrics (Proposed)

  • Reduce scam incidents by 40%
  • Increase verified listing clicks by 30%
  • Decrease lease misunderstanding disputes

Wireframes & Flows

High-fidelity Screens

Reflection

Designing for students requires balancing trust signals with a lightweight experience. Next steps include a usability test with 8–10 students and integrating campus housing resources for verification flows.
